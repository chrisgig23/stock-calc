What Is WealthWise?
In short: WealthWise is a personal portfolio tracker that helps you understand what you own, what it's worth, and how it's performing — across all of your investment accounts in one place.

📊 It's a portfolio tracker

You log your stock and fund holdings, and WealthWise calculates your current value, cost basis, and profit or loss — all in real time using live market prices.

🗂️ It's account-aware

You can set up multiple accounts (e.g. Roth IRA, brokerage, paper trading) and track each one separately, or view everything together from the Dashboard.

📈 It tracks history

WealthWise saves daily snapshots of your portfolio's total value so you can see how it has grown (or changed) over time on the Portfolio Growth chart.

🎯 It supports target allocations

You can set a target percentage for each ticker in an account. WealthWise shows you where you are vs. where you want to be, so you know when to rebalance.

🚫 It's not a brokerage

WealthWise doesn't execute trades. Schwab users can sync data automatically; for other brokerages, you enter data manually or via CSV. Think of it as your personal ledger — it does the math.

🔒 It's just for you

Your data is private to your account. You can create multiple user profiles if needed, but there's no social or sharing layer.

Getting Started
1

Create an account

Click + Add Account in the sidebar. Give it a name that reflects what it represents — your brokerage name, strategy name, or whatever makes sense to you (e.g. "Fidelity Roth IRA", "Long-Term Growth", "Paper Trading").

2

Add your holdings

Navigate to the account and open Edit Portfolio. Enter each ticker you currently hold, along with the number of shares and your total cost basis (what you paid for them). You can also import positions from a CSV if you're moving from another tool or exporting from your broker.

3

Log your transactions (optional but recommended)

For a more accurate picture over time, record your individual buy and sell transactions. This powers the Portfolio Growth chart and lets you track realized gains. You can import transactions in bulk from a Schwab CSV export.

4

Set target allocations (optional)

If you follow a specific investment strategy, go to Allocation for that account and set a target percentage for each holding. WealthWise will show you how far off you are and what that gap looks like in dollar terms.

5

Check the Dashboard

Once you have holdings in at least one account, the Dashboard gives you a cross-account overview — your total portfolio value, an allocation pie chart, growth over time, and your top performers at a glance.

Key Concepts

These are the terms you'll see throughout WealthWise and what they actually mean.

Term What it means
Holding A position you currently own in a specific ticker within an account. Defined by a ticker symbol and number of shares.
Cost Basis The total amount of money you originally paid for a holding — including all purchases. This is your "break even" reference point.
Market Value What your holding is worth right now, calculated as: shares × current price. Prices are pulled live from Yahoo Finance when you refresh.
Unrealized Gain/Loss The difference between your current market value and your cost basis. It's "unrealized" because you haven't sold yet — it's a paper profit or loss.
Realized Gain/Loss Profit or loss you've actually locked in by selling shares. Recorded via transactions and visible in the Reports section.
Average Cost Your cost basis divided by the number of shares held. Useful for understanding your effective buy-in price across multiple purchases.
Target Allocation The percentage of an account's total value you want a specific ticker to represent. Set by you under Allocation — WealthWise doesn't rebalance automatically.
Portfolio Snapshot A saved record of your account's total market value on a specific date. These snapshots are what power the Portfolio Growth chart over time.
Transaction A logged event — a buy, sell, dividend, interest payment, fee, or transfer. Transactions are the raw history of activity in an account.
Account A container for holdings and transactions. Think of it as mirroring a real-world brokerage account, strategy, or fund. You can have as many as you need.
Features

The Dashboard is your home base. It aggregates data across all your accounts and shows:

  • Stat cards — Total portfolio value, total cost basis, and total unrealized gain/loss
  • Allocation pie chart — How your money is spread across tickers, by current market value
  • Portfolio Growth chart — Your portfolio's total value over time, built from daily snapshots. Requires at least one saved historical data point.
  • Top Performers chart — Tickers ranked by unrealized % gain, showing your best and worst movers
  • Per-account cards — A quick summary of each account's value and gain/loss with a link to dig in

Prices on the Dashboard refresh each time the page loads. For the most current numbers, use the Refresh Market Data button inside an account.

Inside any account, the Positions view shows each holding with its current price, market value, cost basis, average cost per share, unrealized gain/loss, and percentage change. This is the most detailed per-account view of your current state.

Use Edit Portfolio to add, update, or remove holdings. Use Refresh Market Data to pull the latest prices and save a new portfolio snapshot for that day.

Transactions give you a full, timestamped log of everything that has happened in an account — buys, sells, dividends, interest, fees, and transfers. Recording transactions is the most accurate way to track your true cost basis and realized gains over time.

You can add transactions manually or import them in bulk. WealthWise supports Schwab CSV exports out of the box — go to Import → Transactions inside an account and upload your export file.

Note: transactions are a log and don't automatically update your holdings. After importing, review your positions under Edit Portfolio to make sure shares and cost basis are current.

The Allocation view lets you define how much of an account you want allocated to each ticker, expressed as a percentage. WealthWise then compares your target with your current actual allocation and shows the gap — in both percentage points and dollar terms.

This is most useful if you follow a strategy like "60% equities, 30% bonds, 10% cash" or a specific index-fund model portfolio. After market movements drift your allocations, the gap view tells you exactly what needs to be bought or sold to rebalance.

Targets must sum to 100%. You'll see a warning if they don't.

The Reports section provides a deeper breakdown of account performance — including realized gains and losses from closed positions, income from dividends, and a summary of fees and other activity.

Reports are built from your transaction history, so the more complete your transaction log, the more useful this section becomes. If you've only entered holdings without transactions, some report sections will be empty or limited.

WealthWise supports both direct Schwab API sync and manual CSV import.

Schwab Direct Sync (recommended) — Connect your Schwab account once in Settings and sync positions and transactions automatically with one click. No CSV downloads needed. Go to Settings → Connect Schwab Account to set it up.

CSV Import — If you prefer manual control or use a brokerage other than Schwab, you can import from CSV in two ways:

  • Import Positions — Upload a CSV of your current holdings to populate an account in bulk. Useful when first setting up or migrating from a spreadsheet.
  • Import Transactions — Upload a Schwab transaction history export. WealthWise will parse it and add the transactions to your account's log.

To export from Schwab: log into your Schwab account → History → select the date range → Export. Download the CSV and upload it here.

Duplicate detection is built in — if you import the same file twice, previously imported transactions won't be added again.

You can create as many accounts as you need. Some common ways people use multiple accounts:

  • Mirror each real brokerage account separately (Fidelity, Schwab, Robinhood, etc.)
  • Segment by strategy (Long-term buy-and-hold vs. active trading vs. speculative)
  • Track a paper trading account to test ideas without real money
  • Separate tax-advantaged accounts (Roth IRA, 401k) from taxable accounts

The Dashboard always shows the combined picture across all accounts. Each account's detail view shows just that account. You can reorder accounts in the sidebar using the edit (pencil) button.

Prices are refreshed each time you load a page or click Refresh Market Data. WealthWise does not push live price updates in the background.

Going forward, snapshots are saved each time you click Refresh Market Data inside an account. Doing this regularly (weekly or after market movements) keeps your growth chart up to date.

You can use WealthWise with just holdings (simpler), just transactions (more complete), or both. Using both gives you the richest picture.

Tips & Best Practices

🔄 Build and maintain your growth chart

Click Build Growth Chart on the Dashboard once to backfill history from your transactions. After that, click Refresh Market Data weekly to keep adding new snapshots going forward.

🎯 Keep cost basis accurate

Your unrealized gain/loss is only as good as your cost basis data. After any purchase, update your holding's cost basis to include the new shares.

🗂️ One account per real account

For the cleanest reporting, mirror your real brokerage accounts 1:1. It makes the Reports section much easier to reconcile with your actual statements.

📥 Import after big moves

After an active trading period, do a Schwab CSV export and import transactions in bulk rather than entering each one manually. It saves a lot of time.

🧮 Use allocations for strategy

Even if you don't rebalance frequently, setting target allocations and checking the gap view occasionally is a good discipline. It helps you spot when drift has moved you away from your strategy.

📱 Collapse the sidebar

On the Dashboard, click the ‹ button at the top of the sidebar to collapse it and give your charts more room. Your preference is saved so it persists across page loads.
